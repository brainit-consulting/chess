BrainIT Chess Engine

Why this project exists

The BrainIT Chess Engine began with a simple but ambitious idea: build a chess engine that thinks visibly, explains its decisions, and scales its strength in a transparent, principled way.

Today, the engine is a clean, classical alpha–beta implementation with shared rules, a shared evaluation function, and multiple difficulty modes controlled purely by search depth and time.

This makes the engine understandable, hackable, and an ideal foundation for deeper research and experimentation.

With its current architecture, the engine can realistically approach strong club to expert‑level play. Reaching beyond that — toward master and grand‑master‑level engines — requires collaboration.

We’re inviting builders, not spectators

We’re looking for programmers who are excited by:

  • Search optimization (move ordering, pruning, transposition tables)
  • Evaluation design (positional heuristics, balance, long‑term planning)
  • NNUE or hybrid neural evaluation approaches
  • Opening theory integration and automated book building
  • Endgame tablebases and perfect‑play transitions
  • Performance tuning in constrained environments (browser, workers, time budgets)
